Introduction

The cryptocurrency market has witnessed a series of key events that have driven its growth in recent years. The accelerated approval of exchange-traded funds (ETFs) has not only introduced new liquidity to the market, but also broadened the audience base of cryptocurrencies. Monetary policy adjustments in major economies around the world, especially the Fed's interest rate cuts, have reduced the cost of funds for cryptocurrency investment, thereby stimulating wider market participation. At the same time, the halving of Bitcoin production has further highlighted its scarcity, driving up prices and deepening the capital concentration in the market.
